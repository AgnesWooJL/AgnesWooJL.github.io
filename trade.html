‚òï The global significance of coffee

Coffee is the beverage that is consumed second only to water and tea in the world. Every day, more than 2 billion cups of coffee are consumed globally. Its trade chain covers multiple countries and economies including planting, processing, transportation, reprocessing and consumption. It is a huge system involving millions of farmers and billions of dollars of circulation.

üåç The global coffee trading chain

The main production of coffee is concentrated in tropical climate countries such as Brazil, Vietnam and Colombia, while the consumption market is mainly in Europe, America and East Asian countries, presenting a pattern of "production in the south and sales in the north". Some non-origin countries (such as Germany and Switzerland) also play the role of transit and export countries in global trade due to their strong processing and packaging capabilities.

üåç Asymmetries in the Global Coffee Value Chain

Global Value Distribution

Producing countries like Brazil, Vietnam, and Colombia focus on raw coffee exports, while developed countries dominate roasting, branding, and retail‚Äîcapturing most of the value.

The Four Coffee Waves

Each wave represents disruptive changes in sourcing, roasting, and distribution‚Äîdeepening market control by global giants.

Market Concentration

Over 80% of green coffee is traded globally, with just six companies controlling around half of all transactions. Prices are volatile, largely set on the New York Stock Exchange.

Trade Barriers

Emerging countries face multiple obstacles: tariffs, technology gaps, national taxes, and branding challenges, making it hard to move up the value chain.

Value Disparity

Farmers receive less than 1% of a cup‚Äôs retail price; in 2002, producers earned under $6B from a market worth over $60B.

Arabica vs Robusta

Arabica is valued for flavor and used in specialty coffee (mainly from Brazil); Robusta, produced largely by Vietnam, is used in instant and blended products.

Timeline: Why does the price of coffee change?

2020: Extreme Climate

The frost and drought in Brazil have led to a 30% reduction in production, directly pushing up the futures prices.

2021: Freight rates soared

During the epidemic, global logistics were strained, which raised the costs of transportation and imports.

2022: Financial Speculation

The inflow of funds into the commodity market has intensified the price fluctuations of coffee futures.

‚òï The Global Journey of a Cup of Coffee

The coffee beans used in the latte you had for breakfast might have originated from Minas Gerais, Brazil. They passed through Rotterdam Port, were processed in German bakeries, and then entered the Asian market. This process spanned across three continents and six links, forming a typical global value chain.
